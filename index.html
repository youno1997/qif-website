What happens on your phone, stays on your phone

Qif uses Apple's own Screen Time technology to pause apps — it can never see your screen, your messages, or what you do inside any app. There's no login, no account, and nothing is collected or sent anywhere. Your location is used only to calculate prayer times, on-device.

Questions

Good to know

Is Qif free?+
Qif offers a 3-day free trial, then an affordable subscription that supports development. And if you genuinely can't afford it, you can unlock full access for free — we only ask that you make a dua. Nobody is priced out of guarding their prayers.
Can Qif see what I do on my phone?+
No. Qif is built on Apple's Screen Time (FamilyControls) framework, which lets an app pause other apps without ever seeing their contents. Qif can't read your screen, your messages, or anything inside the apps you lock. Nothing leaves your device.
What if I have a real emergency and need an app?+
You're never locked out. An Emergency Pause in Settings instantly suspends all blocking for one hour, and you can mark a prayer as completed or snooze at any time. Qif is a commitment you make to yourself — you always hold the keys.
Which apps can I lock?+
Any apps, whole categories (like Social or Games), and even websites — you choose, using Apple's standard picker. Most people start with three to six of their most distracting apps.
Do I need to keep Qif open?+
No. Once you've set it up, the locking happens automatically in the background at prayer times and your daily limit, even when Qif is closed.
What devices does Qif work on?+
Qif is available for iPhone on the App Store and requires Screen Time / Family Controls, which is built into iOS.
